My go-to fried rice recipe

Posted in Recipes by Elisa on Oct 2, 2020

Did you make too much rice with your dinner last night? I’m always excited when this happens (in fact, I often do it on purpose) because in our house that can only mean one thing: fried rice!

Jump to Recipe

I love fried rice because it’s delicious and making it is a great way to get rid of leftover bits and pieces from the fridge. In the video below, I found some enoki mushrooms that I had already used most of, and some frozen beans that my mom had cooked earlier in the week (I used them in place of frozen peas). She does this sometimes with vegetables that are nearly over-ripe and which she doesn’t have any plans for. Storing them in the freezer pre-cooked means that she can save some time on prep later down the track when she’s cooking another meal. It’s pretty genius if you ask me.

Most of the other vegetables in this recipe are long-lasting, so we almost always have them on hand; carrot, cabbage and onion all store well and can be used in so many different dishes. But you can really make this recipe with any vegetables you like. As for the protein, I’ve used chicken in this version but you can replace it with whatever you fancy. Bacon, ham or seafood would work well – you may just have to alter the place in the recipe in which you add them.

I’ve also made this fried rice with chickpeas and replaced the oyster sauce with another sweet, Asian-style sauce like hoisin or sweet chili to make it vegetarian. Similarly, it can be easily converted to a gluten free recipe with a few adjustments to the sauce: you can get gluten free oyster sauce and the soy sauce can be replaced by tamari.

Above is a video of me making this recipe. Don’t expect any fancy filmography (and my cooking techniques will probably make my chef friends cringe) but it shows the general process I follow and gives a visual representation of the instructions I’ve written below. I personally find this very useful whenever I’m reading recipes online. If nothing else, it’s a pretty chill watch!

Fried rice

This is my go-to recipe because the effort required to deliciousness achieved ratio is right in my sweet spot. It tastes like takeaway but I know exactly what's in it, and I make it with leftovers. What's not to like?

Equipment

  • wok

Ingredients
  

  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2-4 eggs beaten
  • ~2 tbsp neutral-flavoured oil (like vegetable, canola, sunflower, or rice bran)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves chopped
  • a similar volume ginger crushed
  • 1 onion diced
  • 1-2 chicken breasts cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 carrots diced
  • 1 small head of broccoli cut into small florets
  • 1-2 cups cabbage chopped
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 3 cups day-old rice refrigerated

Sauce (mix together in a small bowl)

  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Optional

  • 1-2 handfuls spring onions/coriander chopped
  • 1 handful mushrooms chopped
  • 1 small can pineapples chopped

Instructions
 

  • Heat your butter in the wok – as I discovered when filming my video, it gets hot fast! Make sure you move the wok a little to get it up the sides so you have room to move the eggs around than I did. Scramble the eggs until they are just cooked, then move them to a bowl and set aside.
  • Next, heat the neutral-flavoured oil in the wok and add the garlic, ginger and onions. Fry them until the onions have just started turning translucent. Then add the chicken, and mix everything until the chicken appears white on the outside.
  • Add vegetables in order of how dense they are. The more dense a vegetable, the longer it will take to cook. So in this instance I would add the broccoli and carrot first, and stir fry. If they're taking a while and your arm is getting tired you can turn off the heat and put a lid on for 5-10 mins. The risidual heat that's trapped inside should steam them nicely. When they are starting to soften, add cabbage, frozen peas, and mushrooms, and stir fry.
  • When all of the vegetables have softened, it's time to add the rice! If it's coming straight from the fridge it'll be in a big lump that you need to break up.
  • Stir until everything is well-combined and almost all of the pieces of rice have been separated, then pour over your sauce mix.
  • Add pineapples and herbs at this point if you're using them. Mix until everything has been evenly coated, and heated through.
  • Serve! This is obviously a rice-heavy meal so I often eat it with a side of miso or corn soup, or some extra steamed vegetables.
