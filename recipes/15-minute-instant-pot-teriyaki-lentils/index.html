Instant Pot ‘Teriyaki’ Lentils

Posted in Recipes by Elisa on Oct 27, 2019
Jump to Recipe

This is one of the easiest recipes I’ve made for the Instant Pot, and we eat it often for lunch or dinner. With a few simple sides (often things that we have left over in the fridge) it’s a pretty nutritious meal, and it’s one of my go-tos for when I can’t be bothered making anything fancy.

That being said, it can be dressed up a lot if served with different things, or seasoned in additional ways. It has a great umami flavour that is somewhat reminiscent of real teriyaki, but is different enough to be its own tasty thing.

My recipe is inspired by one I found on a Youtuber called Cheap Lazy Vegan’s website. You can read it here. If you want to make my recipe on the stove top, you can use her recipe as a reference to convert it.

Cheap Lazy Vegan also has some super delicious, healthy and extremely easy recipes – definitely worth checking out if you’re interested in reasonably well-balanced vegan food.

Instant pot ‘teriyaki’ lentils

These super quick and easy teriyaki lentils are delicious served with rice or potatoes, and sauteed greens. This recipe is vegetarian, but could easily be converted to make it vegan, or gluten free, with a few ingredient substitutions.
Print Recipe
Prep Time 5 mins
Cook Time 10 mins
Servings 2

Equipment

  • Instant Pot

Ingredients
  

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion
  • 250 mls dried red lentils
  • 400 mls water
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar

Instructions
 

  • Dice onions. I like to cut them somewhere between a regular and a fine diced size. Is there a word for this?
  • Turn Instant Pot on to 'Saute' mode and add olive oil. Allow it to heat for a minute or so.
  • Add onions and saute until they start changing colour, then turn off the Instant Pot. They don't need to be completely cooked through.
  • Add all of the remaining ingredients and mix.
  • Put the lid on the Instant Pot and make sure that the pressure valve is turned to the 'Sealing' position.
  • Press 'Pressure Cook' and change the timer to 2 minutes.
  • When the time is up and the Instant Pot beeps, quick release the pressure by carefully turning the pressure valve to 'Venting'.
  • Mix the lentils before serving with some kind of carbohydrate (we like steamed potatoes or white rice) and some sauteed vegetables (I often go for beans or leafy greens and carrots).
